The Rijpwetering bridge on the **high-speed rail line (HSL)** was showing deformations, prompting an analysis of its current and future safety against the European norms and standards.

Several non-linear models with different load configurations and varying spring constants were built to study how forces transmit through the structure. I used **Python to parse the XML output** from the SCIA Engineer models, then combined the forces to determine the enveloping tension in the concrete pile foundation, allowing the piles to be verified with section-by-section unity checks.

This one sits right at the intersection of my two careers: structural engineering problem, solved with code.
